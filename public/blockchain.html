What Is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that securely records transactions across a network of computers. Each transaction is stored in a "block" and linked to previous ones, forming a tamper-proof "chain." This ensures that once data is recorded, it cannot be altered, providing immutability and security.

Blockchain is transparent, allowing all participants in the network to view the transactions. While commonly associated with cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, blockchain has applications in various industries such as finance, supply chain, and healthcare.

Solana Blockchain

Solutions.

Solana is a high-performance blockchain designed for decentralized applications (dApps) with fast transaction speeds and low fees. It uses a unique Proof of History (PoH) consensus mechanism, allowing it to process up to 65,000 transactions per second. Solana`s architecture is highly scalable and does not require layer-2 solutions, making it ideal for applications like DeFi, gaming, and NFTs.
